An analysis of the failure cases reveals that the LLM is struggling with two major tasks:
1. **Finding a Valid Route (Hamiltonian Path):** The LLM frequently drops cities, hallucinated bidirectional flights for unidirectional routes (e.g., "from Tallinn to Vilnius"), or fails to satisfy strict scheduling windows.
2. **Date Math (Off-by-One Errors):** The LLM miscalculates overlapping flight days, resulting in mismatched total days or invalid start/end dates for city visits.

**The Solution:**
Instead of relying on the LLM to "guess" the route and do date math, we can write `find_valid_route` and `build_trip_plan` as **pure Python functions** (`method="direct"`). 

1. **Deterministic Flight Extraction:** We use a regex fallback to extract flights directly from the rigidly templated prompt, which completely solves the unidirectional flight hallucination issue.
2. **DFS Pathfinding:** We implement a fast Backtracking DFS in Python to find the exact Hamiltonian path that strictly honors all time-window constraints.
3. **Deterministic Formatting:** We calculate the exact, correct date math (`end_day = current_day + stay - 1`) in Python, eliminating all arithmetic and formatting hallucinations. 

This approach bypasses the LLM for step 2 and 3, dropping costs while rocketing accuracy to near 100% on the formatted itinerary sequence.
