An analysis of the failure cases reveals a consistent pattern in how the LLM deduces the murderer:

1. **What SPECIFIC failure pattern did you identify?**
   The current `deduce_murderer` prompt explicitly commands the LLM to *"Weight physical evidence heavily (fingerprints, DNA, weapon possession)"*. In the MUSR dataset, access to the weapon or possessing physical evidence is roughly a 50/50 split — half the time the murderer has the obvious weapon, and half the time the innocent suspect has it (or is framed). By weighting physical evidence heavily, the LLM is basically flipping a coin or getting trapped by the obvious weapon-holder. 

2. **Why did the previous iteration fail?**
   Iter 1 correctly identified this issue but overcorrected by telling the LLM that physical evidence is a "red herring". This inadvertently trained the LLM to *penalize* the suspect with the weapon, which caused it to fail the 50% of cases where the true killer *does* hold the weapon!

3. **What is the MINIMAL change that fixes this pattern?**
   Instead of forcing the model to heavily weight *or* penalize physical evidence, we must instruct the LLM to treat physical evidence **neutrally**. The definitive heuristic in MUSR murder mysteries is that the true murderer's statement always contains a **logical contradiction** against a subtle background fact (e.g., claiming to see something in the dark, or staying dry in the rain). We will re-orient the prompts across the three extraction and deduction steps to hunt for these factual impossibilities rather than fixating on the weapon.
