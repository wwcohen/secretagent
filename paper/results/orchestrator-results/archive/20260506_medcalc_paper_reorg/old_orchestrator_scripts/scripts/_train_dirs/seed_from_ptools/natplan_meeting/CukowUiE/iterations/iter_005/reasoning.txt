1. **What SPECIFIC failure pattern did you identify?**
The pipeline correctly formats responses and tracks time reasonably well, but the LLM in `plan_visit_order` is selecting sub-optimal schedules. Specifically, it:
a) Repeatedly stops planning early (missing late-night friends).
b) Greedily schedules long meetings (105-120 minutes) that block the schedule and prevent visiting 2-3 shorter meetings, thus reducing the total *count* of friends met.
Additionally, `build_meeting_plan` occasionally hallucinates "You wait until {time}." when it arrives *after* the availability has already started.

2. **How many of the shown failures share this pattern?**
All 12 failures exhibit sub-optimal sequence planning (producing valid schedules, but missing 1 or 2 friends compared to the golden plan). Several specifically drop friends who are available late in the evening, or waste massive chunks of the afternoon on a single long meeting instead of grouping multiple short ones.

3. **What is the MINIMAL change that fixes this pattern?**
I updated the docstring prompts for both `plan_visit_order` and `build_meeting_plan`. 
- For `plan_visit_order`, I explicitly instructed it to construct *ONE* highly-optimized timeline (removing the confusing instruction to evaluate multiple paths), to beware of long 105/120 minute meetings skipping them if they block multiple shorter ones, and re-emphasized never stopping early. I also reminded it that travel times are asymmetric.
- For `build_meeting_plan`, I clarified the AM/PM transition rules and explicitly stated that arrival at e.g., 1:15PM is *after* 12:45PM, so no wait line should be printed. 

4. **Why will this change NOT break currently working cases?**
It strictly improves the LLM's heuristics for the greedy scheduling task using known optimal strategies (Earliest Deadline First + shortest duration priority). It keeps all Python orchestration logic and regex parsing intact, avoiding the brittleness of pure Python solving. The changes gently steer the LLM's CoT to avoid its biggest pitfalls.
