1. **Specific failure pattern identified:** The LLM fails to recognize that characters who are "engrossed", "engaged in a conversation", or "immersed" do not witness objects being moved by others, leading to incorrect "true belief" answers instead of "false belief" answers. It also sometimes forgets that characters who move an object themselves obviously know where they put it.
2. **How many failures share this pattern:** Almost all 33 failures feature one of these exact distraction keywords or involve a self-move. 
3. **Minimal change that fixes it:** Augment the `narrative` string passed into `raw_answer` with 5 crisp "Theory of Mind Rules" that explicitly link those distraction keywords to a failure to observe.
4. **Why it won't break working cases:** We are not changing the pipeline flow, not adding risky regex parsing, and not changing the output structure. The injected rules simply act as an attention guide for the LLM to read the story more accurately. For cases where a character *is* watching, the rules explicitly confirm they know the new location, preventing over-correction.
