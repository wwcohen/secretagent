1. **What SPECIFIC failure pattern did you identify?**
The previous iteration overfitted the training set by hardcoding specific string matches (e.g., `'strong rapport'`, `'almos' means almost non-existent`). This caused a drop in evaluation accuracy (from 73.3% to 70.0%). Furthermore, the final decision LLM was overly relying on the AI-generated JSON summaries (`team_data` and `task_data`), which frequently hallucinated constraints (like fabricating a lack of upper-body strength for Paul in ex052) or missed the critical fact that certain individuals clashed with *both* of their teammates.

2. **How many of the shown failures share this pattern?**
Nearly all failures reflect a misalignment in handling trade-offs between individual skills and team dynamics. Cases like `ex019`, `ex021`, `ex096`, `ex005`, and `ex091` all feature one universally toxic person who clashes with BOTH teammates; the ground truth requires isolating this person to the solo task, but the model failed to do so. Other cases (`ex046`, `ex052`) highlight the importance of recognizing *hard* physical constraints versus *soft* skill weaknesses when making trade-offs.

3. **What is the MINIMAL change that fixes this pattern?**
I have removed the overfitted, hyper-specific string constraints from the prompt. Instead, I replaced them with a robust, hierarchical 5-step Chain-of-Thought Rubric.
- First, I explicitly warned the LLM that `team_data` and `task_data` are *AI-generated summaries* and instructed it to prioritize the raw narrative to avoid acting on hallucinated constraints.
- Second, I formalized the **Isolation Rule** (if someone clashes with both others, they MUST be solo) and the **Synergy Rule** (pair positive relationships).
- Third, I clarified the **Trade-off Rule**: it is usually correct to assign someone to a task they are bad at (a soft constraint) to avoid a toxic pairing or honor the isolation rule, provided a *hard physical constraint* isn't broken.

4. **Why will this change NOT break currently working cases?**
It perfectly encapsulates the dataset's core logic without relying on memorized text snippets. By instructing the model to prioritize the raw narrative over the intermediate JSON summaries, we protect against upstream hallucinations. The rules are logically sound, universally applicable to team allocation tasks, and explicitly tell the LLM *how* to weigh competing constraints rather than just listing them.
