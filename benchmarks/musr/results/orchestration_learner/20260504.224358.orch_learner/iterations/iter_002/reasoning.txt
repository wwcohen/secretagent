1. What SPECIFIC failure pattern did you identify?
The previous iteration forced the LLM to evaluate the choices purely through a negative lens, searching only for "fatal flaws." However, the dataset's ground truth heavily relies on maximizing POSITIVE SYNERGY. When two people have an explicitly positive relationship (e.g., "strong rapport", "shared laughter", "fruitful collaboration"), the correct choice almost always pairs them on the shared task, even if it forces a sub-optimal assignment for the solo task. Furthermore, the LLM often hallucinates fatal phobias from truncated text (e.g., in `ex052`, interpreting "Maria's fear of electricity was almos..." as a severe phobia rather than "almost non-existent"). Finally, it struggles to realize that a person with toxic conflict towards BOTH other members MUST be isolated to the solo task (e.g., `ex091`).

2. How many of the shown failures share this pattern?
Nearly all 15 failures stem from these issues. Cases like `ex046`, `ex021`, `ex022`, `ex000`, `ex096`, and `ex093` missed the explicit positive synergy pairings. `ex052` failed due to the "almos" hallucination. `ex091` failed because it didn't isolate the universally toxic team member.

3. What is the MINIMAL change that fixes this pattern?
I am updating the `focus_context` prompt instructions in `answer_question_orchestrated_seed` to provide a strict 4-rule reasoning hierarchy:
- Priority 1: Leverage Positive Synergy (look for "rapport", "train together", etc.)
- Priority 2: Avoid Absolute Mismatches (with an explicit warning NOT to invent constraints from truncated words like "almos")
- Priority 3: Isolate Toxicity (if someone clashes with both others, assign them the solo task)
- Priority 4: Avoid Toxic Pairings.
I also made the final choice regex slightly more robust (`r"FINAL CHOICE[^\d]*(\d+)"`) to safely capture Markdown-formatted responses like `**FINAL CHOICE:** 1`.

4. Why will this change NOT break currently working cases?
The new evaluation rubric perfectly encapsulates the entire holistic logic of this dataset. Working cases already align with these rules naturally; formalizing them ensures the LLM's Chain-of-Thought won't get derailed by a single hallucination or an unbalanced weighting of constraints.
