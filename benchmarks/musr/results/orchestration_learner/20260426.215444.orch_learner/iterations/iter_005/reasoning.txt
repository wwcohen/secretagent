An analysis of the failure logs reveals two distinct but compounding problems causing the pipeline to fail on the training set:

1. **Extraction Bug (Numbered Lists)**: In `deduce_murderer`, the LLM has been outputting its step-by-step reasoning using numbered lists (e.g., "1. Means", "2. Motive"). When the text is passed to `extract_index`, its fuzzy-matching fallback often gets confused by these digits, accidentally parsing "1." as the multiple-choice index 1, even if the LLM's actual textual conclusion favored the suspect at index 0. 
2. **Reasoning Flaw (Weapon Red Herrings)**: The LLM frequently gets distracted by a suspect possessing a "similar" weapon (like a gym trident or a gardening pickaxe) and chooses them over the suspect who possesses the *exact* murder weapon described in the narrative (e.g., the *antique* trident, or the pickaxe bought *that exact day*).

### The Fix:
1. **Bulletproof Parsing**: I added a safe Regex parser to the Python workflow blocks. It searches exclusively for the "Final Answer: <Name>" string and extracts the exact choice index. If it finds it, `extract_index` is bypassed entirely, preventing the numbered-list bug. If it fails, it safely falls back to `extract_index`.
2. **Explicit "Exact Match" Extraction**: I added targeted instructions to `extract_suspects_and_evidence` and `verify_alibis` to explicitly track the `murder_weapon`'s exact description and verify if a suspect's weapon is an "EXACT match" or just a "replica/similar".
3. **Prompt Hardening**: I instructed `deduce_murderer` to use bullet points instead of numbers to aid the extraction logic, and to actively compare weapon exactness. 

These changes are highly surgical, adding robust fallback logic without altering the successful CoT structure from Iteration 3.
