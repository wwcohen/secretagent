1. **What SPECIFIC failure pattern did you identify?**
   The pipeline frequently predicts the wrong suspect by simply picking the person who owns the murder weapon or has the most obvious physical evidence. In puzzle datasets like MUSR, physical evidence is often deliberately planted as a red herring to frame the innocent suspect. The previous prompt explicitly instructed the LLM to "Weight physical evidence heavily (fingerprints, DNA, weapon possession)", which actively forced it to fall for the red herrings.

2. **How many of the shown failures share this pattern?**
   Almost all of them. For example, in ex084 (Bruce owns the hatchet), ex083 (Bridget practices with a crossbow), ex045 (Frederick owns a sai), ex053 (Lucy uses a shiv), the LLM incorrectly predicted the suspect who owned the weapon, completely missing the logical alibi contradictions of the true murderer.

3. **What is the MINIMAL change that fixes this pattern?**
   Rewrite the docstrings (prompts) for `verify_alibis` and `deduce_murderer`. Remove the instruction to weight physical evidence heavily. Instead, explicitly warn the LLM that physical evidence is often a red herring, and instruct it to find the "logical lie" — a contradiction between a suspect's alibi and subtle background facts (like weather, closed locations, or broken items).

4. **Why will this change NOT break currently working cases?**
   It correctly aligns the LLM's reasoning with the actual logical structure of the MUSR dataset. Working cases that didn't rely on red herrings will still be solved correctly because the true murderer always has the contradicted alibi. This simply prevents the LLM from being baited by surface-level keyword matches for weapon possession.
