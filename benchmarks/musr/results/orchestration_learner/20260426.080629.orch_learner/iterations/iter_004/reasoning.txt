An analysis of the execution traces reveals two distinct failure mechanisms that are preventing the pipeline from reaching higher accuracy:

1. **Output Truncation (The Primary Bug):** In 12 of the 14 failure cases shown, the output of `deduce_murderer` was cut off mid-sentence (e.g., `...the physical evidenc [$0.0009]`). Because the reasoning block was truncated, the model never generated the `FINAL ANSWER: <suspect name>` line. The fallback logic then fed the incomplete, half-baked reasoning block to `extract_index`, forcing it to blindly guess which suspect the truncated text was leaning towards.
2. **Logical Traps in Physical Evidence:** The pipeline frequently fell for "red herring" physical evidence. For example, in **ex022**, the murder weapon was a pickaxe lodged in the victim's body. Penelope was suspected because she had a pickaxe, but she explicitly possessed hers in her yard. The model failed to realize that if she still has her pickaxe, it cannot be the one lodged in the body. Furthermore, the model occasionally mixed up facts between suspects (e.g., in **ex024**, attributing Russell's bleach burns to Alice).

### The Fix
1. **Prevent Truncation:** Instruct `deduce_murderer` to keep its reasoning explicitly concise (maximum 150 words). This forces the LLM to complete its Chain of Thought and output the `FINAL ANSWER` safely within the token limit.
2. **Inject "Weapon Logic" and Fact-Checking:** Add explicit fields to `extract_suspects_and_evidence` and `verify_alibis` to track the location of the murder weapon vs. the suspect's personal weapon. Prompt `deduce_murderer` to use this logic to clear suspects who couldn't have left their weapon at the scene.
3. **Pass Full Context:** Update `answer_question_workflow` to pass the structured `evidence` into `deduce_murderer` so the final synthesis step has the fully extracted means, motives, and physical states, rather than just the alibi analysis.
