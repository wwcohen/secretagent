An analysis of the failure cases reveals two critical issues driving the errors in the pipeline:

1. **Forced Over-Weighting of Red Herrings:** The current `deduce_murderer` prompt explicitly instructs the LLM to *"Weight physical evidence heavily (fingerprints, DNA, weapon possession)"*. In MUSR mysteries, both suspects typically have access to the weapon, or the true murderer frames the innocent suspect. The strict instruction forces the LLM to overrule its own logical findings. For example, in **Case ex002**, the LLM correctly identified that Marianne possessed "guilty knowledge" (knowing the manner of death before being told), but it explicitly overruled this finding to select Paige simply because Paige's connection to the weapon was "direct and physical."
2. **Hallucinations to Satisfy Prompts:** Because the model feels compelled to build a strong case based on physical evidence, it occasionally hallucinates facts to justify its choice (e.g., in **Case ex045**, it hallucinates that Jerome was "spotted purchasing multiple sais," which never occurred in the text).
3. **Environmental Logic Flaws:** The zero-shot reasoning struggles with state-based deductive logic. In **Case ex086**, the model flagged Grace's wet, muddy clothes as suspicious for a murder that happened *indoors* at a fitness center, failing to realize that her muddy clothes actually supported her alibi of being out hunting in the wilderness.

### The Fix
We will refine the prompts for `verify_alibis` and `deduce_murderer` to realign the LLM's deductive logic without breaking the existing pipeline structure:
- **Remove the explicit command to heavily weight physical evidence.** Instead, instruct the model to treat weapon access neutrally, explicitly warning it *not* to let weapon possession override a clear logical contradiction.
- **Emphasize "Guilty Knowledge" and Environmental Contradictions.** Add specific prompt instructions teaching the LLM to cross-reference physical states (wet/dry) against the environment (indoors/outdoors), and to hunt for verifiable lies or guilty knowledge.
- **Add a Strict Anti-Hallucination Constraint.** Force the model to rely *strictly* on the narrative to prevent it from inventing weapon purchases.
