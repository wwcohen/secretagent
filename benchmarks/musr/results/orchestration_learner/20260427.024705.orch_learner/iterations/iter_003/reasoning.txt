1. **Specific failure pattern identified**: The zero-shot model systematically prioritizes individual skills over interpersonal conflicts, whereas the MUSR dataset treats conflicts as strict hard constraints. In nearly all 19 failures, the model incorrectly paired two people who explicitly dislike/argue with each other into a 2-person task, or failed to isolate a globally conflicting "toxic" person into a 1-person task.
2. **How many failures share this pattern**: Nearly all of them. This is the central latent structure of the team allocation dataset.
3. **Minimal change**: To capture the "simulate with thinking" CoT performance referenced in the docstring (84%) without risking the 0.0% crash regressions of Iterations 1 & 2, I defined a new `@interface` function `holistic_think_and_solve`. It explicitly instructs the LLM to write out its CoT and then append the final choice after a `CHOICE:` marker. I then updated the orchestrator to perform a safe string split on `CHOICE:` to pass *only* the final choice string to `extract_index`. I also added a bulletproof `try/except` block that falls back to the original `raw_answer` pipeline (with a strong conflict-avoidance hint injected) if anything fails. 
4. **Why it won't break currently working cases**: 
   - It doesn't use risky `re` imports (which caused Iteration 2's 0.0% crash).
   - It strips out the verbose CoT before passing the string to `extract_index` (which prevents the extraction failures that caused Iteration 1's 0.0% crash).
   - The fallback ensures that the absolute worst-case scenario defaults back to the working pipeline.
   - It cleanly respects the task structure without overfitting to specific names or roles.
