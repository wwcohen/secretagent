An analysis of the failure cases reveals a consistent structural flaw in the orchestrated workflow: **`compute_answer` is blind to the original question and table.** 

Because it only receives `operation` (e.g., "sum", "max", "comparison") and the extracted `values` (e.g., `['$0.55', '$0.70']`), it has no way to know:
1. That it needs to multiply those prices by quantities specified in the question (e.g. "buy 6 items").
2. That a "max" operation was asking for the *name* of the person/day (argmax), not the maximum numeric value.
3. What the values represent for comparisons (e.g. comparing demand vs supply to output "surplus" instead of just evaluating raw numbers).
4. That it's a stem-and-leaf plot requiring stem/leaf concatenation.

To fix this systematically without blowing up the workflow control flow, we can make a **targeted surgical change**:
1. Update `compute_answer`'s signature to take `question` and `table` for full context, and instruct it on how to handle ambiguities (argmax, purchasing multiples, budgets).
2. Update `identify_operation` to include missing operations (`median`, `argmax`, `multiply`) in its hint list.
3. Update `extract_relevant_values` to encourage extracting quantities from the question itself.
4. Update the three workflows that call `compute_answer` to pass the newly required `question` and `table` arguments.
