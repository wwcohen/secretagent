An analysis of the failure cases and the rollback of Iteration 1 reveals the following:

1. **The root cause of failures**: Most failures occur because `compute_answer` only receives a single word `operation` (like "sum" or "max") and a list of `values`, but it lacks the contextual quantities, entities, or rules defined in the original question. As a result, it fails on "argmin/argmax" questions (returning the value instead of the label), quantity questions (failing to multiply by quantities like "1/5 of a pound"), and multi-step logic (like shortage/surplus).
2. **Why Iteration 1 crashed (0.0%)**: Iteration 1 attempted to fix this by modifying `compute_answer` to `def compute_answer(question: str, operation: str, values: list[str])`. Changing the positional order of arguments for a ptool without correctly updating every internal call—or if the evaluation framework tests ptools dynamically based on their old signature—causes a fatal `TypeError` across the board. 
3. **The safe fix**: We will pass the question to `compute_answer` to provide the needed context, but we will do it by safely appending `question: str = ""` to the end of the signature. This is strictly backwards-compatible. We will update the explicit workflow logic to pass the question.
