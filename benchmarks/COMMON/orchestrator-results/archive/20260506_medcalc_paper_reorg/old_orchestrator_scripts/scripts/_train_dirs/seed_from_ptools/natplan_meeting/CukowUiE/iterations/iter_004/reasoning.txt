1. What SPECIFIC failure pattern did you identify?
The LLM consistently fails to include late-night or narrow-window friends in its schedules inside `plan_visit_order`. It greedily picks early friends and then simply "stops" planning too early in the evening (around 6-8 PM), abandoning optimal paths despite having ample time remaining in the day.

2. How many of the shown failures share this pattern?
Almost 100% of the failures shown share this exact flaw (e.g., Case 622 drops William and Ashley who are available after 9:15 PM; Case 940 drops Kevin at 8:45 PM; Case 614 drops Melissa at 9:30 PM). 

3. What is the MINIMAL change that fixes this pattern?
- **Python orchestration side:** Intercept the extracted `info_json` and computationally sort the `friends` list chronologically by their `available_from` time (and then `available_to`) before passing it into `plan_visit_order`. LLMs process text top-to-bottom, so presenting options chronologically inherently guides them to a mathematically superior packing strategy. I also added robust markdown block stripping to prevent `json.loads` failures.
- **Prompt docstring side:** Added specific "CRITICAL RULES" to the `plan_visit_order` docstring, explicitly instructing the LLM not to stop early, to check late-night availability (8:00 PM - 10:30 PM), and notifying it that the list is conveniently pre-sorted chronologically.

4. Why will this change NOT break currently working cases?
The extraction step (`parse_meeting_info`) and step-by-step verification step (`build_meeting_plan`) are untouched. The core orchestration workflow is entirely preserved. `build_meeting_plan` still verifies validity and drops anyone that doesn't mathematically fit. The LLM simply receives a cleaned, chronologically sorted JSON dictionary which requires less cognitive overhead to parse.
